Term I Financial Requirements

Introduction

As part of our mission to support education for vulnerable children, the Lead Initiative is preparing for the 2025–2026 academic year, Term I. In this new phase, we aim to expand our reach by supporting 144 students, with the long-term goal of extending assistance to more than one school.

The financial requirements outlined below reflect the essential educational needs of each student, alongside transportation costs necessary for effective program implementation and monitoring.

Program Objectives for Term I

  • Provide comprehensive educational support to 144 vulnerable students
  • Ensure 100% retention rate throughout the term
  • Maintain regular monitoring and evaluation visits to partner schools
  • Deliver essential learning materials and nutritional support
  • Build sustainable partnerships with additional educational institutions

Student Support Components

Each supported student receives a comprehensive package that addresses both immediate educational needs and basic welfare requirements. This holistic approach ensures that financial barriers do not prevent children from accessing quality education.

The support package includes school fees coverage, essential learning materials such as notebooks and writing instruments, basic stationery supplies, and nutritional support through biscuits and other snacks that help students maintain energy levels throughout the school day.
